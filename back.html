Categories / Categorias de Kanji

Kanji can be classified into four main categories. / Kanji pode ser classificado em quatro categorias principais.

① Pictographic characters Originated from the pictures of objects. (ex. 山 川 馬 雨). / Caracteres pictográficos.
② Indicative kanji Made of points and lines to indicate abstract ideas. (ex. 一 三 上 下). / Kanji indicativo.
③ Compound kanji Composed by combining pictographic kanji and other types. (ex. 林 男 明). / Kanji composto.
④ Phonetic-ideographic kanji Formed by combining phonetic and ideographic elements. (ex. 時 持). / Kanji fonético-ideográfico.
Note: Most kanji are formed by combining kanji characters. Learning the first 300 kanji thoroughly will help make it easier to study the other kanji characters. / A maioria dos kanjis é formada combinando caracteres. Aprender bem os primeiros 300 facilitará o estudo dos demais.
How to write kanji strokes / Modo de escrever os traços do kanji

There are some rules for writing kanji. / Existem algumas regras para escrever kanji.

1. Horizontal lines Draw horizontal lines from left to right. / Horizontais da esq. para a dir.
2. Vertical lines Draw vertical lines from top to the bottom. / Verticais de cima para baixo.
3. Squares How to draw a square. / Como desenhar um quadrado.
4. Stroke ending: 3 basic ways There are three ways to end a stroke. / Terminação: 3 formas básicas.
1 top
2 hook
3 stretch
